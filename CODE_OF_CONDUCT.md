# Code of Conduct

Sapien is built on a simple principle: honest disagreement
is more valuable than polite agreement.

This code of conduct reflects that principle.

---

## Our Standards

**Be honest.**
Say what you actually think. If you believe part of the
architecture is wrong, say so clearly and explain why.
Vague politeness helps nobody. Honest criticism helps everyone.

**Be specific.**
Vague criticism — "this won't work" — is not useful.
Specific criticism — "this won't work because the DAG retrieval
latency will exceed X at Y scale, as shown by Z" — is valuable.
Make your criticism actionable.

**Challenge ideas, not people.**
The architecture can be wrong without the person who proposed it
being wrong. Disagree with concepts, proposals, and claims.
Do not attack contributors personally.

**Credit others.**
If your contribution builds on someone else's research or idea,
cite them. If your contribution is original, claim it.
Attribution matters in research.

**Acknowledge uncertainty.**
Nobody knows how to build human-like intelligence. If you claim
certainty where none exists, you are not contributing honestly.
"I think" and "the evidence suggests" are more honest than
"this is definitely true."

**Welcome beginners.**
Not every contributor will have a PhD or industry experience.
A 10th-grade student with genuine curiosity and honest thinking
is more valuable than a credentialed expert who refuses to
question their assumptions. Judge contributions on their merit,
not their author's credentials.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Personal attacks or insults directed at contributors
- Dismissing contributions based on the contributor's background,
  age, education, or affiliation rather than the content
- Claiming credit for others' work
- Deliberately misrepresenting the architecture or others'
  contributions
- Spam, self-promotion unrelated to Sapien research,
  or off-topic content

---

## Enforcement

This project is currently maintained by one person.
Enforcement is simple: contributions that violate this code
of conduct will be removed. Repeated violations result in
being blocked from the repository.

---

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces including
GitHub issues, pull requests, the Forestritium Discord
Sapien channel, and any other spaces where Sapien is discussed
under the project's name.
